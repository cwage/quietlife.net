I have a problem with reading books. I have a mild form of ADD when it comes to reading, so I jump around a lot, and find myself reading 4-5 books at once, none of which I ever actually finish. I also buy books compulsively at a faster pace than i could realistically read them.

So, I've decided to do two things:

  1. Finish all the books I have started but not finished. This starts with [The Marx-Engels Reader][1], which I am reading right now.
  2. Read all of the books we own before I allow myself to buy another book.

Here's a list of books I have started in the last few years but never finished that I intend to finish:

<!--more-->

  * [The Marx-Engels Reader][1]
  * <strike>[Good Muslim, Bad Muslim][2], by Mahmood Mamdani</strike>
  * [Reconstructing the Classics: Political Theory from Plato to Marx][3]
  * [The Affluent Society][4]
  * [The Prince][5]
  * [Class][6]
  * [Secrets and Lies][7]
  * [By-Line Ernest Hemingway : Selected Articles and Dispatches of Four Decades][8]
  * [The Federalist Papers][9]
  * [The Anti-Federalist Papers][10] -- I am at least fair and balanced in my half-reads
  * [The Looting of Social Security][11]
  * [The Truly Disadvantaged][12] -- I only read about 75% of this, and for as much as I reference it, I owe it to myself to read it again and finish it this time.
  * [Full Spectrum Disorder: The Military in the New American Century][13]
  * <strike>[From Beirut to Jerusalem][14]</strike>
  * [The Calculus of Consent: Logical Foundations of Constitutional Democracy][15]
  * [The Meaning of Relativity][16]
