We saw it tonight. It was good, entertaining, but not great. I thought the original was better. Spoilers below:

<!--more-->

The exposition into Wonka's past was unnecessary, and ruined what was otherwise an enigmatic mad-hatter type mystery figure. This fatal mistake really ruins the ending. What is all this family crap? In the original, the ending crystalized nicely in a brief moment of "whoa, what a dick" panic when Wonka flips out on Charlie. Then it quickly flops back as you realize it was all just a test by the lovable (yet still ambiguously insane) Willy Wonka. Instead, in the remake, we get Wonka as an actual human being, with a past, and feelings, and actual character development. If there's one thing people **hate** when they see movies, it's actual human beings. The whole prolonged change of heart ending really blew it for me. I also mostly agree with [NK's review][1].

Sorry, Johnny, but Wilder retains the Best Wonka award:
