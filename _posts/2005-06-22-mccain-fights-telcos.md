Kudos to John McCain for breaking from his party's fine tradition of corporate favoritism and co-sponsoring a bill to [promote municipal ISPs][1]:

> Sens. John McCain, R-Ariz., and Frank Lautenberg, D-N.J., Wednesday will introduce legislation that would permit municipalities to offer low-cost broadband service – putting the legislators at odds with SBC Communications and other telecom giants that oppose such networks.
> 
> Their measure, dubbed the “Community Broadband Act of 2005,” would serve as a counterweight to a bill offered in late May by Rep. Pete Sessions, R-Texas, that would bar such municipal networks in areas where private companies offer high-speed Internet access. 

McCain here poses as an admirable counter-weight to the [corporate shill, Pete Sessions][2].
