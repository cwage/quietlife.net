I'd encourage any of you that are concerned about the destruction of tent city to come out for this tomorrow afternoon:

> WHO: CONCERNED CITIZENS FOR TENT CITY, cctcNashville@aol.com, 615-474-8390  
> WHAT: A RALLY TO SAVE TENT CITY  
> WHERE: THE STEPS OF THE METRO COURTHOUSE  
> WHEN: THIS FRIDAY, OCTOBER 24, FROM 3:00 PM TO 5:00PM  
> WHY: PROVIDING & IMPROVING A PLACE TO CALL HOME IS BETTER THAN DESTROYING IT. 

Briefly, Tent City is an encampment of around 30 or so (I think -- varying, as well) people that Metro PD is attempting to shut down due to recent increased visibility around a stabbing. They are citing "violence" and "public health" as factors for clearing the camp. I am not certain where they expect these people to go. There's an article [in the Tennessean][1] and [the Scene][2] about the situation.

There's some [great info here][3] about the role of these sorts of encampments using info from Seattle. Metro should be more engaging in finding a way to make this encampment legal and safer, not razing it.

Stop by and/or help spread the word.. Thanks!
