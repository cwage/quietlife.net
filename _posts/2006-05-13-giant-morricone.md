[Joe Hisaishi][1]'s score to [Spirited Away][2] is one of my all-time favorite pieces of music. While listening to something completely different this evening, I was sharply reminded of it. The piece? A brief interlude in Ennio Morricone's score for Cinema Paradiso. Compare this, the third interlude from "Four Interludes":

\[http://www.place.org/~cwage/sounds/Ennio\_Morricone\_Third_Interlude.mp3\] (Ennio Morricone - *Four Interludes*)

to this snippet from *A Road to Somewhere* from the Spirited Away score:

\[http://www.place.org/~cwage/sounds/Joe\_Hisaishi\_Road\_to\_Somewhere.mp3\] (Joe Hisaishi - *Road to Somewhere*)

Is it just me, or are the similarities striking? I find myself wondering if Hisaishi wasn't directly inspired by Morricone here. In any event, they are both two very beautiful pieces of music.
