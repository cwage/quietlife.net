A chain of thought I had this morning:

  * [Twitter][1] is fun and (tentatively) tremendously useful, albeit [difficult to explain][2]
  * Twitter is a centralized proprietary service, and thus prone to issues of scaling (outages), and privacy.
  * Twitter is not magic technology --[XMPP and SMS gateways could basically do everything it does now][3].
  * That's nice and all, but Twitter has the user-base.
  * [XMPP][4] has a track record of being Really Cool Technology that languishes in obscurity due to its lack of user-friendly implementations and wide user-base adoption. 
      *   * [Google][5] saved XMPP from a similar languishing death in the world of Instant Messaging by adopting this open standard as the basis for [Google Talk][6].
          * **Conclusion:** Google needs to re-factor Google Talk to be/add a twitter-alike service.</ul> 
        Maybe?
