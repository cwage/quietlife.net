Some brief observations about things people are saying about [Dennis Kucinich][1], and his campaign for president.

<!--more-->

<p style="font-weight: bold">
  Dennis Kucinich is unelectable.
</p>

<p style="margin-left: 10px;">
  Granted. Kucinich is probably unelectable, yes. This doesn't mean he doesn't deserve support.
</p>

<p style="font-weight: bold">
  Kucinich is too ideological. He couldn't run the country. He couldn't make the necessary compromises
</p>

<p style="margin-left: 10px;">
  This is quite an insult, considering who is running the country at the moment. Kucinich is ideological, but he's also a politician. He's already proven he is capable of doing that job. Ideology has become a dirty word in our lexicon these days, but let's not forget that ideology isn't necessarily bad. Being <b>blinded</b> by ideology is bad. There was a time when we elected presidents because they stood for something. And now we're going to dismiss someone because he is willing and able to take a stand?
</p>

<p style="font-weight: bold">
  Kucinich flip-flopped on abortion.
</p>

<p style="margin-left: 10px;">
  So what?
</p>

<p style="margin-left: 10px;">
  From <a href="http://www.commondreams.org/headlines03/0223-05.htm">http://www.commondreams.org/headlines03/0223-05.htm</a>:
</p>

<p style="margin-left: 20px; font-style:italic">
  "This isn't something that I arrived at overnight," said Kucinich, who is methodical in his approach to every issue, from war in Iraq to his opposition to genetically modified foods.
</p>

<p style="margin-left: 20px; font-style:italic">
  "I don't believe in abortions, few do," he said. "I do, however, believe in choice."
</p>

</span>

<p style="margin-left: 10px;">
  I am not so cynical that I can't give him the benefit of the doubt. Kucinich has sacrificed his political career for his ideology before. (See <a href="http://www.lessig.org/blog/archives/001424.shtml#001424">this post</a> by Kucinich on Lessig's blog discussing his battles with power deregulation in Cleveland, for an example.) I don't think he would take this stance if he hadn't honestly arrived at this conclusion.
</p>

<p style="margin-left: 10px;">
  If I am wrong, and he is merely being politically opportunistic, then so be it. So what? If, hypothetically, he gets elected, and flip-flops yet again, he'd have hell to pay. But in the grand scheme of things, abortion is a small slice of the political pie. I'd take a pro-life, but otherwise progressive liberal Kucinich in office over a decidedly moderate centrist Howard Dean.
</p>
