So, I got my camera fixed. Canon has redeemed itself. I had a brief scare a few days ago when the lens cover stuck briefly, but it seems okay now. So far so good. I've been taking pictures again, as a result. Some of my favorites below the fold:

This picture was even darker, pre-GIMP fixup, but I really like the sky, even at the expense of the underexposure on the rest. Some post-fixup with colour balances helped bring back some of the foreground.

[<img src="http://static.flickr.com/51/150798332_4cfa332360.jpg" width="500" height="375" alt="Thistle" />][2]

I wish the flower itself was in better focus, but I love how the sky turned out looking more gray than blue -- a nice contrast with the purple of the flower.

As far as my general photo-taking experience so far: I am realizing I am already leaning on two crutches: First is taking pictures at night, which just seems easier in general -- f/8.0 with a long exposure and you get something cool. Second is my tripod, which was a great buy, but isn't something I can carry everywhere. I need to get better at framing and stuff so I can take interesting pictures even in normal light at a faster exposure.

Taking pictures of people is very, very hard. People move. Why won't they just sit still? I am starting to see how a bounce-flash would be handy for taking pictures of people. A head-on flash is not exactly the most flattering light for an intimate portrait, and people move too much for a longer exposure without a flash. Anyways, I think I have about covered round 1 of Beginner's Landscape photography, and I need to start focusing on more useful and practical photography skills: taking pictures of people, and things during the day.
