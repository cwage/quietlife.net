I think they'd revoke my membership to nerddom if I didn't blog the party last night. It was fun -- Since I didn't actually go to the actual conference I was just there to crash the party and meet people, and that's what I did:

<!--more-->

Right off the bat, I talked a bit with [MMMikey][1], who later hooked me up with **FREE** tickets to see Todd Rundgren and Joe Jackson on Monday (score!). I also met [Tall Paul][2], who I had talked to on the phone before, as he's a recent and seemingly happy client of [Swirbo][3]. Talked with Bruce Barry a bit when the Scene folks arrived -- he's as nice in person as he is online. I think he and Amanda chatted it up quite a bit while I made the rounds.

I met the famous [Les Jones][4] and his lovely wife Melissa, which I was looking forward to -- he's got [his recap][5] of the event as well. We were both there earlier so we sat around and talked as we scanned the crowd for familiar faces that don't exist. Eventually I retrained myself to scan the nametag first.

Finally met [Blake][6] briefly, but he was hard to nail down. He's here in Nashville though, so i'm sure i'll run into him again.

Met some of the other Nashville crew: [muffy][7] and [caruthers][8], [saucylibrarian][9] and a thoroughly unaffiliated (with blogging) friend.

One of the Nashville bloggers I didn't get to meet was [Kevin][10], who was **there**, but I just kept missing him.

Also talked with [Pink Kitty][11] briefly, who I think is trying to recruit Amanda for some bellydancing stuff.

Talked a bit with [Chris Muir][12], and also ran into my other favorite cartoonists, [Cox and Forkum][13]. We talked a bit about our little [altercation][14], but wisely avoided the actual source of our .. disagreement. Forkum, to his credit, was as civil and pleasant as a gentleman should be, and we talked about something we have in common -- cars (the [new miata][15], to be specific).

Of course, I also met a bunch of new very nice people, none of whose names I could remember now if my life depended on it.

Had an interesting conversation with Roger Green. Roger is the founder and president of [Ipswitch][16]. If Ipswitch doesn't ring a bell, their two flagship products probably will: [WS-FTP][17] and [What's Up][18]. WS-FTP was and is one of the most popular FTP clients around, and anyone who has ever downloaded a pirated copy of Adobe Photoshop 4.0 is probably familiar with the hax0red version of his software (what was that called again? Uhm, not that I ever used it). We talked a bit about [Swirbo][3] and open-source software in general. An interesting conversation I may rehash in detail later on the [CentreSource Blog][19].

We closed out the night chattin with [Tim Morgan][20], who was a pretty funny guy, [Mr Roboto][21], and a lovably belligerent drunken stranger who was very good at pretending he had any idea who I was.

Thanks to our gracious hosts for a great time!

UPDATE: Oh, and kudos to to [Jacob's Posse][22] for doing a great job at the door. They only sent me home to change twice and for $20 they let me keep my Gatt.
