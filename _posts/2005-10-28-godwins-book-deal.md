Can you violate [Godwin's Law][1] with an entire book? [Apparently so.][2].

Via [Matt Welch at Hit&Run][3], who includes a good bit from an older post where he echoes [my thoughts][4] as well on the contextless abuse of Orwell in co-opting and applying the term "fascist" to the anti-war left:

> It used to be that Hitch deployed this [*Partisan Review*][5]-style vocabulary to convince his old Internationalist comrades that Yugoslavia was the next [Spanish Civil War][6]. But now it's just a violate-Godwin's-Law-for-free card, which a grateful pro-war nation has [embraced][7], providing yet more evidence that there is no Lefty rhetorical trope the Right will despise enough to avoid [co-opting completely][8].
> 
> Such deft rhetorical switchery and political shape-shifting leads to a lot of unintentional comedy, and here's my favorite: Hitchens' biggest new fans are especially fond of extrapolating from George Orwell's [old saw][9] about pacifism being "objectively pro-fascist," sometimes as [headlines][10] on links to Hitch's latest. But as [Eric Blair's modern popularizer][11] knows too well, Orwell -- whose original essay, it should be mentioned, was referring to British pacifists during Hitler's bombing siege of London -- [repudiated][12] his "objectively pro-fascist" line before the War was even over, in an [essay about propaganda][13] that's well worth your time.
