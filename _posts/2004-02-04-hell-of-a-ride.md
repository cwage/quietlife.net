Well, there's no picture of the day feature, so here's a picture of the year. This is a ride you won't find at any amusement park. Click to view the full-sized (1260x1017, 238KB) image:

[![][1]][2]

For those of you that are bandwidth-endowed, check out the 3008x1960, 2.8MB version [here][3]

The picture was taken during an air show at Mountain Home Air Force Base, Idaho, when the pilot, Chris Stricklin, initiated a split-S too low in an F-16. For more on the background, see [avweb's story][4], and also [the confirmation][5] of authenticity as well as some other movies.. Pretty amazing.

The picture was taken by Staff Sgt. Bennie J. Davis III, Still Photographer, U.S. Air Force, with a Nikon D1X camera using a 300-mm lens with an aperture setting of 2.8 and shutter speeds of 1/1000 and 1/2000. The picture is admirable, not only because of the quality, but also because the F-16, after being diverted away from the crowd of 68,000+, was headed right for the tower, stopping only a few hundred feet away.
