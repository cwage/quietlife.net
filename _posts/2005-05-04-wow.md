*Backdrop: in 1984, the Union Carbide (now a subsidiary of Dow) pesticide plant in Bhopal [leaked forty metric tons of methyl isocyanate][1], killing around 15,000 people and injuring hundreds of thousands, making it probably the most deadly industrial accident ever.*

How to [make a company look bad][2]:

> On December 3, 2004, the twentieth anniversary of the disaster, a man claiming to be a Dow representative named "Jude Finesterra" was interviewed on the BBC. He claimed that the company had agreed to clean up the site and compensate those harmed in the incident. ([video][3]) Immediately afterward, Dow's share price fell 4.2% in 23 minutes, for a loss of $2 billion in market value.[2] Dow quickly issued a statement saying that they had no employee by that name —that he was an impostor, not affiliated with Dow, and that his claims were a hoax. BBC broadcast a correction and an apology. The statement was widely carried. [3] 

I have to say, that is a pretty damn awesome prank. Evidently, before this occurred, there was [a documentary][4] made about these guys in 2003. I've added it to our Netflix queue -- it looks pretty interesting.
