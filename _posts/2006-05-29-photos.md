In which I meticulously, obsessively fret over the details of my latest picture-taking expedition (at [Cheekwood][1]).

Translation: photowankery follows:

<!--more-->

[<img src="http://static.flickr.com/49/156029343_a7686e7775.jpg" width="500" height="443" alt="img_1434" />][2]

This picture pisses me off -- it's alright, but not great. I had to crop it because it was framed badly to begin with. I had \*the perfect\* picture right after this: the statue was framed perfectly with the bamboo in the background -- my fourth or fifth attempt, where i finally got it. And it was blurry. I just didn't check the results on this one because I was sure I had it (it was at an angle where I couldn't see the viewfinder or LCD). Curses.

[<img src="http://static.flickr.com/66/156028977_4757ac2f4e.jpg" width="500" height="375" alt="img_1431" />][3]

I had some White Balance Issues while taking these pictures. I have tried using the white-balance auto-detect feature on my camera by finding something suitably white and using that functionality. What I've learned is that the white-balance on the LCD can look a lot different than the results it yields. A lot of these pictures were super-green and a bit washed out, even after my best attempt at correcting the color balance in GIMP.
